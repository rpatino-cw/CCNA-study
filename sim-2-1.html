Why VLANs exist

A switch by default flattens everything into one broadcast domain. VLANs cut a switch into virtual switches. Two ports in VLAN 10 talk; a port in VLAN 10 cannot reach a port in VLAN 20 — different LANs sharing the same metal. Across switches you need a trunk: one wire that carries every VLAN, with each frame stamped (tagged) so the far switch knows which VLAN it belongs to. The native VLAN is the exception — it travels untagged. Mismatch the native VLAN on each end and you create a security bypass (VLAN hopping).
