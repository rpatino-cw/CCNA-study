VLAN Tagging Sim

Configure ports as access (one VLAN) or trunk (carries many, tagged). Send a frame and watch the 802.1Q tag get added at the trunk and stripped at the far access port. The point: VLANs only stay separate because tags travel with the frame.

Why VLANs exist

A switch by default flattens everything into one broadcast domain. VLANs cut a switch into virtual switches. Two ports in VLAN 10 talk; a port in VLAN 10 cannot reach a port in VLAN 20 — they're different LANs sharing the same metal. Across switches you need a trunk: one wire that carries every VLAN, with each frame stamped (tagged) so the far switch knows which VLAN it belongs to. The native VLAN is the one exception — it travels untagged. Mismatch the native VLAN on each end and you create a security bypass (VLAN hopping).
