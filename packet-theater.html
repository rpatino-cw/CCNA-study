Four fundamental topologies — watch how packets route through each one.

Star

Central switch connects all hosts. Single point of failure at center, but easy to troubleshoot. Most common LAN topology.

Bus

Shared backbone cable. All hosts hear all traffic — collision domain. Legacy 10Base-2/5. Terminated at both ends.

Ring

Token passes around the circle. Only the token holder transmits. Used in FDDI and Token Ring. Predictable latency.

Full Mesh

Every device connects to every other. Maximum redundancy, but O(n²) links. Common in WAN cores and spine-leaf fabrics.

Collision — two frames on one wire

Collision (CSMA/CD)

On shared media (hubs, 10Base-5), two hosts transmit simultaneously. Signals overlap and corrupt. Both detect the collision, send a jam signal, then back off for a random delay (binary exponential backoff) before retrying. Modern switched networks eliminate collisions — each switch port is its own collision domain.
