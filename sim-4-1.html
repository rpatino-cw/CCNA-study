NAT in Motion animated

Why NAT exists: IPv4 is exhausted. Private RFC-1918 hosts (10/8, 172.16/12, 192.168/16) can't be routed on the public internet. NAT rewrites packet headers at the border so many private hosts share one or a few public addresses. Pick a mode, fire packets, watch the header rewrite mid-flight at the router.
