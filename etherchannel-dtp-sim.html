EtherChannel + DTP Simulator

Pick a mode on each switch and watch the link react. EtherChannel decides whether ports bundle; DTP decides whether a link becomes a trunk. Same exam trap underneath both: two passive sides never agree.

The one-line answer

EtherChannel: at least one side must actively initiate. LACP needs an active; PAgP needs a desirable. Two listeners (passive+passive, auto+auto) never bundle. Never mix protocols, and on only talks to on.

DTP: dynamic desirable asks to trunk; dynamic auto only waits. auto+auto falls back to access. trunk+access = mismatch.

🧠 auto + auto = access (both wait, nobody asks) — the DTP twin of LACP passive+passive. desirable actively asks → trunks with anything dynamic or trunk. trunk + access = mismatch (VLAN leak / errors). switchport nonegotiate stops DTP frames — use on hard-set trunk/access and on links to routers, servers, firewalls, or non-Cisco gear.
