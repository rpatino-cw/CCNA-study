IPv6 Multicast Scope & Groups

All multicast addresses start with ff. The 4th hex digit (T-flag/scope) defines how far the packet propagates — interface, link, site, or global.
WHY this exists. Multicast lets one sender reach many receivers without sending the same packet repeatedly. The scope field stops packets from leaking. Routing protocols use well-known groups: OSPFv3 talks to ff02::5, EIGRP to ff02::a. ND replaces ARP via solicited-node multicast. Pick the scope wrong and your packet either dies on the wire or floods globally. Pick the group right and protocols just work.
