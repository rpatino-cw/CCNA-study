Walkthrough
Wireless channel cheatsheet
  • 2.4 GHz: 11 channels (US), each 22 MHz wide @ 5 MHz spacing → ONLY 1, 6, 11 don't overlap.
  • 5 GHz: 24+ channels, each 20 MHz wide and ALL non-overlapping. Bonded into 40/80/160 MHz for higher throughput.
  • 5 GHz UNII bands: UNII-1 (36-48), UNII-2A (52-64, DFS), UNII-2C (100-144, DFS), UNII-3 (149-165).
  • DFS: Dynamic Frequency Selection. AP must listen for radar ~60s before transmitting. If radar detected, must vacate.
  • 6 GHz (Wi-Fi 6E / 7): 1200 MHz of new spectrum, 59 × 20 MHz channels, 7 × 160 MHz channels. Indoor-only in many regions (LPI rules).
  • Channel width vs throughput trade: Wider channel = more throughput but more interference. 80 MHz needs 4× cleaner spectrum than 20 MHz.
