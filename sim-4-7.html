QoS Forwarding Sim

All packets are not equal. A 200ms-late voice packet is useless — the call already gapped. A 200ms-late file download is fine. QoS is the system that knows which is which: classify, mark, queue, drop selectively. Without it, a backup job kills your call. With it, voice always finds 60ms of silence.

Without QoS — congested link

✗ Bursty data fills the queue tail-drop. Voice and video sit behind it. Call quality drops. Packets out of order = jitter.

With QoS — classify, mark, queue, drop

✓ Voice goes to the Low-Latency Queue (LLQ — strict priority). Video gets bandwidth guarantee (CBWFQ). Data uses what's left + WRED randomly drops at high fill so TCP backs off gracefully.

The Four Steps

1. Classify

Match traffic on ACL, NBAR, source port, DSCP. Decide who is who.

2. Mark

Stamp DSCP (L3, 6 bits in IP ToS) or CoS (L2, 3 bits in 802.1Q). Trust boundary — usually access switch.

3. Queue

LLQ for voice (strict priority, served first). CBWFQ for video/critical data (bandwidth guarantee). FIFO for default.

4. Drop / Shape

WRED drops randomly before queue fills. Policer enforces rate ceiling. Shaper buffers + smooths.
