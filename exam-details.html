Step 1 of 2

Buy Your Voucher

A voucher is your payment for the exam. You purchase it from Cisco, then use the voucher code when you schedule through Pearson VUE. Buying the voucher and booking the exam are two separate steps.

  • Vouchers are $330 USD and expire 1 year from purchase date
  • Discounts sometimes appear during Cisco Live events or Black Friday
  • Many employers reimburse certification costs — ask your manager or HR before paying out of pocket
  • Cisco also sells bundles (training + voucher) through the Cisco Learning Network Store
  • Do NOT buy vouchers from third-party resellers — they may be expired or invalid

Step 2 of 2

Book Your Exam

Once you have a voucher (or are ready to pay at checkout), schedule your exam through Pearson VUE. You will need a Cisco account linked to your Pearson VUE profile.

  • Testing center: Take it at a physical Pearson VUE location near you
  • Online proctored (OnVUE): Take it from home with a live proctor watching via webcam
  • Book 2-4 weeks out to create a deadline. Don't wait until you feel "ready" — you'll never feel ready.
  • For online: you need a webcam, mic, clean desk, closed room, government ID, and stable internet
  • For testing center: you can compare up to 3 locations for appointment availability

Strategy

Time Management During the Exam

You have 120 minutes for approximately 110 questions. That's roughly 65 seconds per question. Simulations take longer — budget 3-5 minutes each.

Pacing Strategy

First pass: Answer every question you know immediately. Flag anything that takes more than 90 seconds. Move on. Second pass: Return to flagged questions with the time you saved. Important: some question types do NOT allow you to go back.

If you get 3-4 simulation/testlet questions, budget 15-20 minutes total for them. Adjust your pacing for the remaining multiple choice accordingly. Don't spend 10 minutes on one sim — make your best attempt and move on.

Logistics

What to Expect on Exam Day

Testing Center

  • Arrive 15 minutes early. Late = forfeited exam + lost voucher.
  • Bring TWO forms of ID — one must be a government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport). The second can be a credit card, employee badge, or another ID with your name.
  • You'll get a whiteboard and marker (or scratch paper, depending on the center). Practice your subnetting tables and write them out immediately when the exam starts.
  • No phones, smartwatches, notes, food, or drink in the testing room.
  • You'll be assigned a computer in a monitored room with security cameras.
  • Bathroom breaks count against your 120 minutes.

Online Proctored (OnVUE)

  • Log in 30 minutes early to check in, take photos of your room and ID.
  • Clean your desk completely — nothing on it except your computer.
  • Close all other applications on your computer.
  • No other people can be in the room — not even briefly.
  • No talking, reading aloud, or looking away from the screen for extended periods.
  • If your internet drops or the proctor can't see you, your exam may be voided.
  • Use a wired ethernet connection if possible — Wi-Fi drops can end your exam.
The NDA

Before the exam starts, you'll sign a non-disclosure agreement. You legally cannot share specific exam questions with anyone. This is strictly enforced — Cisco has revoked certifications for NDA violations.

Format

Question Types

The exam uses multiple question formats. Knowing what to expect removes surprises.

Multiple Choice
Pick one correct answer from four options. The most common question type. Read all options before selecting — Cisco loves "most correct" answers where two seem right.
Multiple Select
Pick 2-3 correct answers. The question always tells you how many to select (e.g., "Choose two"). You must get ALL selections right — partial credit is not given.
Drag and Drop
Arrange items in the correct order or match pairs (e.g., match protocols to port numbers, or order OSPF neighbor states). Practice these — they feel different from standard MC.
Simulation / Testlet
Given a network topology, answer 3-5 related questions or configure something in a simulated CLI (Packet Tracer-style). These take 3-5 minutes each. You usually cannot go back to previous testlet questions.
Fill in the Blank
Rare but possible. Type a command or value (e.g., a subnet mask, a port number). Spelling and exact syntax matter — show ip route is not the same as sh ip route here.
Results

Scoring

Scores range from 300 to 1000 — not 0 to 100. The passing mark is approximately 825 (Cisco does not officially confirm the exact cutoff). You receive your score report immediately after finishing.

There is a mandatory 5-day waiting period before you can retake the exam. Use those 5 days wisely — your score report tells you which domains were weak. Attack those specifically. You'll need another voucher ($330) unless you purchased a retake bundle.

Preparation

Tips & Tricks

High-Value Topics

  • Subnetting: Memorize the magic number method. You should be able to subnet in under 30 seconds. See the subnetting reference
  • OSPF: Know DR/BDR election rules, cost calculation (reference bandwidth / interface bandwidth), and all neighbor states (Down, Init, 2-Way, ExStart, Exchange, Loading, Full).
  • ACLs: Know where to place standard vs. extended ACLs. Standard = close to destination. Extended = close to source.
  • Wireless: Know WPA2 vs WPA3 differences, CAPWAP tunnel function, and WLC deployment modes (local, FlexConnect).
  • IPv6: Know address types (link-local, global unicast, unique local), EUI-64, and SLAAC vs DHCPv6.

Test-Taking Strategy

  • The exam tests "would this config work?" — not just "what does this command do?" Think like a troubleshooter.
  • Eliminate wrong answers first. Usually 2 out of 4 are obviously wrong — that gives you a 50/50 on tough questions.
  • When unsure, go with the most "Cisco-recommended" answer. Cisco exams favor their best practices.
  • Read EVERY word carefully. Cisco loves tricky phrasing: "which TWO", "all that apply", "MOST likely", "LEAST likely".
  • Write your subnetting cheat sheet on the whiteboard immediately when the exam starts, before answering any questions.
  • Don't change your first answer unless you are certain it's wrong. First instinct is usually correct.
Avoid These

Common Mistakes

Before the Exam

  • Voucher expired before booking. Vouchers expire 1 year from purchase. Buy it when you're 4-6 weeks from your target date, not months in advance.
  • Forgot second ID. You need TWO forms of ID. Confirm you have both the night before.
  • Online proctored environment issues. Background noise, other people in the room, browser pop-ups, unstable Wi-Fi. Test your setup with Pearson VUE's system check tool days before the exam.

During the Exam

  • Running out of time on simulations. Sims look intimidating. Set a hard 5-minute limit per sim — make your best attempt and move on.
  • Not reading "select TWO" and only picking one. If it says pick two and you only pick one, it's wrong. Always check how many answers are required.
  • Spending too long on one question. If you've been on a question for 2+ minutes, flag it and move on. Come back with fresh eyes.

Study Mistakes

  • Over-studying automation (10%) and under-studying routing (25%). Domain 3 (IP Connectivity) is worth 2.5x more than Domain 6 (Automation). Allocate study time proportionally.
  • Cramming IPv6 but forgetting basic switching. VLANs, trunking, and STP are 20% of the exam. Don't neglect Layer 2.
  • Only doing multiple choice practice. If you've never done a drag-and-drop or sim question, you'll lose time figuring out the interface on exam day.
What's Next

After You Pass

Immediate Steps

  • Digital badge from Credly arrives within 24-48 hours. Claim it immediately.
  • Add to LinkedIn — go to your profile, Licenses & Certifications, and add it. Include the Credly badge link.
  • PDF certificate is available to download from the Cisco Certification Tracking System.
  • Physical certificate is mailed to the address on your Cisco account (can take several weeks).

Renewal & Next Steps

  • CCNA is valid for 3 years from the date you pass.
  • Renew by passing any one CCNP core or concentration exam, or by earning Continuing Education (CE) credits through Cisco.
  • CCNP track options: Enterprise, Security, Collaboration, Data Center, Service Provider, DevNet. Pick based on your career direction.
  • The CCNA does not expire while studying for CCNP — passing a CCNP exam also renews your CCNA.

Final Check

Pre-Exam Checklist

Go through this the night before your exam. Check off each item.

1 Week Before

  • Voucher purchased and code saved
  • Exam scheduled (testing center or online)
  • If online: ran Pearson VUE system check successfully
  • Reviewed all 6 domain areas at least once
  • Taken at least one full-length practice exam

Night Before

  • Two forms of ID confirmed and ready (government photo ID + second ID)
  • Know the testing center address and parking / transit plan
  • If online: desk is clean, room is private, webcam and mic tested
  • Subnetting cheat sheet memorized (ready to write on whiteboard immediately)
  • Alarm set — arrive 15 min early (center) or 30 min early (online)
  • No late-night cramming — get a full night of sleep

Exam Morning

  • Eat a good meal — you can't eat during the exam
  • Bring both IDs
  • Leave phone in car / locker (not in the testing room)
  • If online: close all apps, disable notifications, plug in laptop charger
  • Take a deep breath — you prepared for this
