Cisco Wireless Architectures

Three ways to deploy access points. Each splits "where does the brain live?" differently — and that one decision drives config burden, scale ceiling, and resilience. Click each card to see the trade-offs.

The choice that drives everything

An AP does two jobs: RF management (channels, power, encryption, beacons — the radio half) and control (auth, roaming, policy, RRM, monitoring — the brain half). Where you put the brain is the architecture: (a) inside every AP = autonomous, (b) in a central WLC, APs become "lightweight" = split-MAC, or (c) in the cloud = Meraki. Same wireless physics, three management worlds.

Pick an architecture

a · Autonomous

Each AP is its own boss

Every AP runs full IOS, holds its own config, makes its own decisions. No controller needed.
b · Split-MAC (WLC + LWAP)

Central brain, dumb radios

APs are lightweight. WLC holds config, makes decisions. CAPWAP tunnel back to controller.
c · Cloud (Meraki)

Brain in the SaaS dashboard

APs phone home to a Cisco-hosted dashboard. Configure once, applies fleet-wide.
