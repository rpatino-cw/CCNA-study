WHY this matters Every router has one job: pick the next-hop for a packet. The routing table is the answer key. Each row tells you where it learned the route (code), how much it trusts the source (AD), and how far it is (metric). If two protocols know the same destination, AD breaks the tie — not metric, not preference, AD. Memorize this surface and OSPF, EIGRP, BGP, and static all collapse into one decision.
