Two Controllers, Two Scopesdrill

Both are "controllers." Both have GUIs. Both manage Cisco gear. But their scopes are completely different. A WLC manages the wireless plane only — every AP and every wireless client, that's it. Cisco DNA Center is a notch above — it manages the entire campus (wired switches, routers, APs, security policy, segmentation) by intent. You don't tell DNA "set this VLAN" — you tell it "Engineering can talk to Engineering." Read each stem, decide which tool fits.

WLC — Wireless LAN Controller

Manages APs and wireless clients. The brain inside the wireless plane.

  • SSID config, RF channel/power, WPA2/3, RADIUS
  • Fast roaming, mobility groups
  • CAPWAP tunnel termination
  • Wireless-only — no switch, no router, no segmentation

Cisco DNA Center

Manages the entire campus by intent. Wired + wireless + security + segmentation.

  • Intent-based: describe outcome, not config
  • SD-Access fabric, Group-Based Policy, segmentation
  • Discovers + provisions switches, routers, WLCs, APs
  • Assurance, telemetry, automated path-trace
