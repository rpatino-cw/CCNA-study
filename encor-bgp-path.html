Walk the 13-step decision order one criterion at a time. See which attribute eliminates the losers.

Key facts to internalize: Weight is Cisco-local — it is never advertised to any neighbor (not even within your AS). Local-Pref is AS-wide and defaults to 100 — it is carried in iBGP updates only. In practice, most tie-breaking dies at step 4 (shortest AS-Path); Weight and Local-Pref are deliberately tuned, so if they are equal the operator did not set a policy and AS-Path becomes the real decider. Higher is better for Weight and Local-Pref. Lower is better for MED, Router-ID, and Neighbor IP.
