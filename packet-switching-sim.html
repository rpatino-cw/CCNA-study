Why does the internet use packet switching?

Because reserving a dedicated circuit per conversation (like PSTN did) wastes capacity whenever traffic is bursty — which is almost always. Packet switching lets routers statistically multiplex many flows onto shared links, absorbing spikes in queues instead of blocking calls. The tradeoff: variable latency and occasional loss, which TCP compensates for at the endpoints. Modern variants — virtual circuits, MPLS label switching — bolt circuit-like guarantees back on top.

Math

Core formulas

Total delay per hop

d = dtrans + dprop + dproc + dqueue

Four additive components. Transmission = packet size ÷ bandwidth. Propagation = distance ÷ speed-of-light-in-medium.

Little's Law

L = λ × W

Average queue length (L) = arrival rate (λ) × average wait (W). Predicts when routers start dropping.

Transmission delay

dtrans = L ÷ R

Packet length (L bits) ÷ link rate (R bps). A 1500-byte packet on 100 Mbps = 120 µs.

Watch out

Common mistakes

✗"Packet switching has no latency cost."

Reality: Queueing delay often dominates transmission delay. Under load, packets can wait longer in buffers than they spend on the wire.

✗"Circuit switching is obsolete."

Reality: SONET/SDH rings, dedicated lambda services, and some private WAN circuits still use circuit semantics — especially where guaranteed latency matters.

✗"More bandwidth = faster network."

Reality: Bandwidth is throughput ceiling. Bufferbloat, loss, and bad queue management can make a 10 Gbps link feel worse than a well-tuned 100 Mbps one.

✗"MPLS is a tunneling protocol."

Reality: MPLS is a forwarding mechanism — labels replace IP lookups at every hop. Tunnels (VPN, GRE) ride on top of MPLS; they aren't MPLS itself.

✗"Cut-through always beats store-and-forward."

Reality: Cut-through forwards before CRC is verified — a corrupted frame gets propagated. Modern switches use cut-through only when error rates are low and drop to store-and-forward under stress.

✗"Flooding is dumb and never used."

Reality: Flooding is how OSPF propagates LSAs, how ARP works, how STP discovers topology. It's bounded (TTL, split-horizon), not dumb.

In practice

Real-world applications

☎️

Phone networks

PSTN was pure circuit. Modern cellular voice (VoLTE) rides IP packets but reserves QoS slots to preserve call quality.

📹

Video conferencing

Zoom, Meet, Teams use UDP-based RTP with selective retransmit. Pure TCP would create head-of-line blocking.

🏢

SD-WAN

Overlays multiple links, picks best per-packet on loss/latency/jitter. Packet switching with policy.

📧

Email relay

Classic message switching — SMTP store-and-forward. Servers queue the full message until they can hand it off.

🎥

MPLS L3VPN

Carrier VPNs use MPLS labels to keep customer routing tables isolated while sharing backbone capacity.

⚡

HFT fabrics

Cut-through switches hit sub-microsecond port-to-port latency. Every nanosecond matters in financial trading.

🛰️

Satellite relay

High-latency links force TCP tuning — window scaling, selective ACKs, TCP-proxies at the gateway.
