Why powers of 2? Networking is binary — every bit is on or off. So every count of addresses, subnets, or hosts is a power of 2. If 28=256 and 22=4 are reflex-fast, dividing 256 by 4 is just 8−2=6 → 26=64. No long division.

Walkthrough
How the four moves work
  1. Powers of 2. Memorize: 2, 4, 8, 16, 32, 64, 128, 256, 512, 1024, 2048, 4096, 8192, 16384, 32768, 65536. That's 21 through 216.
  2. Value → exponent. See 64? Think "26". See 1024? Think "210". Build the reverse lookup.
  3. Divide via exponent subtract. 256 ÷ 8 → both are powers of 2 → 28 ÷ 23 → 8−3=5 → 25 = 32.
  4. Multiply via exponent add. 32 × 16 → 25 × 24 → 5+4=9 → 29 = 512.
  5. Half-method (fallback). Halve repeatedly when you forget the exponent. 256 → 128 → 64. Each halving = subtract 1 from exponent.
