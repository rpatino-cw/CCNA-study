🌐 OSPF Quick

  • Neighbor states: Down → Init → 2-Way → Exstart → Exchange → Loading → Full
  • DR/BDR: highest priority, tie → highest Router ID
  • RID: manual → highest loopback → highest active IP
  • Hello/Dead: 10 / 40 sec (broadcast)
  • Cost = 10⁸ / bandwidth · area 0 = backbone

🌳 STP Quick

  • Root election: lowest Bridge ID (priority + MAC). Default priority 32768
  • Port roles: Root → Designated → Blocking
  • States: Block → Listen → Learn → Forward
  • PortFast = skip to forwarding (access ports only)
  • RSTP = 802.1w, faster convergence
💡 Plus a quick OSI model dump (Please Do Not Throw Sausage Pizza Away) and wireless encryption (WEP→WPA→WPA2/AES→WPA3) — wireless shows up heavy on the exam.

The 2-Day Plan

Built from real comeback stories on r/ccna. Rule: diagnose the rust, don't relearn everything.

DAY 1 · AM (~3h)

🩺 Full Boson exam — sim mode, timed, NO studying first

This is your rust map. 70s is fine, don't panic. Score it. Write down the per-category breakdown — those low categories are your whole afternoon.

DAY 1 · PM (~4h)

🔧 Patch weak topics only

Review every wrong answer, handwrite notes. Re-lab weak topics. Priority order: STP → OSPF → ACLs → NAT/PAT → VLANs → DHCP → EtherChannel. Then 30 min Anki.

DAY 1 · EVE (~1h)

🔢 Subnetting to reflex

Drill until automatic. Then sleep 7-8h — non-negotiable. The sub is unanimous: sleep beats one more hour.

DAY 2 · AM (~3h)

💪 Second full Boson (fresh exam B/C)

Review wrongs the same way. Score jumps — the 735→900 pattern is common. Learn nothing new today.

DAY 2 · PM (~2h)

🎴 Anki all topics together + re-lab 2 worst

Run full Anki deck mixed (exam-realistic). Re-do your 2 weakest labs once more. Boring = the commands stick.

DAY 2 · EVE

🛑 Stop ~5pm. Relax.

"An hour more of studying won't make a difference." Let it settle.

Exam Traps

Verbatim-sourced from people who just took it. Read twice.

1

Use FULL command syntax in labs. Type interface, not int. Abbreviated gets marked wrong. (exSim graders are strict)

2

Save your labs. Labs carry most of the score. Don't lose points to a missing copy run start.

3

Write your subnet chart during the pre-test instructions. Never subnet in your head under the clock — it'll be staring at you.

4

Some show commands may be disabled to force troubleshooting (one report: show run off on an OSPF item). Don't assume a command is available. [single old anecdote — directional, not guaranteed]

5

Read the full question + all answers. Watch "and/or" wording and OS context (a Linux question wants a Linux command, not Windows).

6

Don't get stuck. OSPF labs eat time — flag and move, come back. Time management fails more people than knowledge.
