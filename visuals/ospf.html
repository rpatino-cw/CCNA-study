Election Rules

1 Highest priority wins. Default priority is 1. Range 0-255.
2 Priority 0 means the router cannot become DR or BDR.
3 Highest Router ID breaks ties when priorities are equal.
4 Non-preemptive: once elected, a DR stays DR even if a higher-priority router joins.
5 If DR fails, BDR is promoted to DR, and a new BDR election is held.

Calculate OSPF Cost

Cost = 100 / 100
Reference BW / Interface BW (rounded down, minimum 1)
OSPF Cost
1
Cisco command: auto-cost reference-bandwidth 100

Common Interface Costs

Interface Bandwidth Cost (ref 100M) Cost (ref 1G)
Serial 1.544 Mbps 64 647
Ethernet 10 Mbps 10 100
FastEthernet 100 Mbps 1 10
GigabitEthernet 1 Gbps 1 1
10GigE 10 Gbps 1 1
Problem: With the default reference bandwidth of 100 Mbps, FastEthernet, GigabitEthernet, and 10GigE all have a cost of 1. OSPF cannot distinguish between these link speeds for path selection.

Recommendation: Change the reference bandwidth to 1000 Mbps or higher on all routers in the OSPF domain.
auto-cost reference-bandwidth 1000
