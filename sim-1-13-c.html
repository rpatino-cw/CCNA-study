Cut-Through vs Store-and-Forwarddrill

A switch can decide where to send a frame as soon as it sees the destination MAC (first 6 bytes) — cut-through, ultra-fast but blind to errors. Or it can buffer the entire frame, run FCS, then forward — store-and-forward, slightly slower but drops bad frames. Fragment-free reads the first 64 bytes (catches collision-era runts) as a middle ground.
