Neighbor Discovery Sim

Cisco devices speak CDP. Everyone speaks LLDP. Toggle protocols per device, then look at the resulting neighbor table — the table tells you which neighbors a device knows about, and gaps in it usually mean someone's running the wrong protocol.

Why this matters

Neighbor discovery is how a switch announces "I'm here, this is my port, this is my model." When you walk into a rack and need to know what's plugged into Gi1/0/24, you don't trace the cable — you ask the switch via show cdp neighbor or show lldp neighbor. CDP is Cisco-only (multicast 01:00:0C:CC:CC:CC). LLDP is IEEE 802.1AB, vendor-neutral. Mixed environments? Run LLDP everywhere. Cisco-only environment? Either works, but LLDP is the safer default going forward.
