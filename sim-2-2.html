802.1Q Bit-Level Inspector

Click each field of a tagged frame to see the bytes. Then walk through DTP modes — including the auto/auto trap that hands attackers a free trunk. The 4-byte tag is small but it's what makes inter-switch VLANs possible.

Why the tag exists

Two switches connected by one cable need to carry frames for many VLANs and tell them apart. 802.1Q solves it with 4 bytes inserted between SRC MAC and EtherType. The receiver sees the tag, learns the VLAN, and either forwards within that VLAN or strips the tag at an access egress. ISL was Cisco's older proprietary version — dead. 802.1Q is IEEE-standard, open, and what every modern switch speaks.
