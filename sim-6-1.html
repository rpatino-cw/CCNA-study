Three planes

Networking has three "planes" of operation. SDN's whole trick: separate the control plane from the data plane, centralize the control plane.

Data plane

The forwarding muscles. Looks at packets, makes per-packet drop/forward decisions. Lives on every device. Stays distributed in SDN.

Control plane

The brain. Builds routing/forwarding tables, runs OSPF/BGP, decides paths. Distributed in traditional, centralized in SDN.

Management plane

Config + monitoring (SSH, SNMP, NETCONF). Can also be centralized — APIs, dashboards, telemetry.
