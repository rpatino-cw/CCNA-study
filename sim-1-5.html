Transport Layer — TCP vs UDP

TCP is a phone call: you say hello, confirm I can hear you, then talk. UDP is a postcard: drop it in the mail, hope it arrives, no callback if it doesn't.
WHY this exists. Two apps need to talk over IP. IP itself doesn't care about order, loss, or duplicates. TCP adds reliability (handshake, sequence numbers, retransmit, ordered delivery). UDP skips all that — it trades reliability for speed. Pick wrong and your video stutters or your bank transfer drops a digit.
