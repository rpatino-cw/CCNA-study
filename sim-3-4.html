OSPFv2 Single-Area Sim

4-router topology in area 0. Step through neighbor states (Down → Init → 2-Way → ExStart → Exchange → Loading → Full). Watch DR/BDR election by priority + router-ID. Compute cost.
WHY this matters OSPF is a link-state protocol. Every router has the same map of the area, then runs SPF (Dijkstra) on it. To get there, neighbors must trade identical link-state databases — that handshake is the seven adjacency states. On a broadcast segment, only the DR and BDR exchange with everyone (avoiding O(n²) chatter). Cost is dead simple: ref-bw / interface-bw. Master these three pieces — states, election, cost — and OSPF stops being magic.
