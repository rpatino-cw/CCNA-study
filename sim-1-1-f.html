Edge vs Middledrill

Endpoints get connected. Network devices do the connecting. A laptop, an IP camera, a printer — these are at the EDGE. They produce or consume traffic. A switch, router, AP, firewall — these sit in the MIDDLE and move traffic. The trap: an AP looks like an endpoint (it's a small box on the wall) but it's actually network gear — it forwards client frames. A server is just a computer, but it's also an endpoint (the role it plays in a flow doesn't change its type — see 1.1.g for that twist). Drag each item into the right bucket.
