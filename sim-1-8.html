IPv6 Compression & Prefix

128 bits, 8 groups of 4 hex digits. Two compression rules: drop leading zeros in each group, and replace the longest run of all-zero groups with :: (only once).
WHY this exists. Full IPv6 addresses are unreadable: 2001:0db8:0000:0000:0000:ff00:0042:8329. Compression makes them tractable: 2001:db8::ff00:42:8329. The rules are strict — only one ::, longest zero run gets it. Get this wrong and your config doesn't parse, your route mismatches, or you mis-read a packet capture.
