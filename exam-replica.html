Exam Tutorial

Read carefully — this will not count against your time

How This Exam Works

  • 102 questions — a mix of multiple choice, multiple select ("choose 2" or "choose 3"), drag-and-drop matching, and simulation testlets
  • 120 minutes — the timer starts when you click "Begin Exam" and cannot be paused
  • Navigation — use Next/Previous to move between questions, or click any question number in the side panel
  • Flag for Review — mark questions you want to revisit before submitting
  • Multiple Choice — click one option. Click again to deselect.
  • Multiple Select — select exactly the number indicated (e.g., "Choose 2"). The question tells you how many.
  • Drag-and-Drop — drag items from the left and drop them on the correct target on the right. Click a filled target to remove the item.
  • Testlets — a network scenario with 3-4 related questions. You cannot go back before the testlet once you enter it.

Exam Strategy

  • Manage your time. 102 questions in 120 minutes = ~70 seconds per question. Don't get stuck.
  • Flag and move on. If unsure, flag it, pick your best guess, and come back later.
  • Unanswered questions are wrong. Never leave a question blank — always guess if you're unsure.
  • Read every word. Pay attention to "MOST likely," "BEST," "FIRST," and "LEAST" — these change the answer.
  • Testlets take time. Budget 3-5 minutes per testlet. Read the topology carefully before answering.
