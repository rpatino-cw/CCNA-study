Reading show interfaces — Five Failure Modesclick an error

Counters tell stories. Late collisions on a full-duplex side = classic duplex mismatch (the other side is half-duplex and stepping on you). CRC errors piling up = bit corruption from a bad cable, EMI, or a flaky SFP. Giants over the 1518-byte baseline = MTU mismatch (often jumbo-frames inconsistency). Runts = under-64-byte frames, often shave from collisions or bad NICs. Speed mismatch rarely links — autoneg usually fails to one side.
