Autonomous vs Lightweight APsdrill

An autonomous AP is its own boss — local config, local SSID, local everything. Fine for one or two APs in a coffee shop. But what if you have 200 APs across a campus? Walking to each one is misery. The fix: split the AP into two halves — a thin radio (LWAP) and a brain (WLC). They talk over a tunnel called CAPWAP. Drop APs onto the floor and watch the topology decide which model can actually exist.
