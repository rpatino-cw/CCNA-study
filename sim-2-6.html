AP Modes — What the Radio Actually Does

Same hardware. Seven different jobs. Pick a mode and watch how the AP behaves: serving clients, sniffing for attackers, surviving a controller outage, or going totally silent.

Why modes matter

A Cisco lightweight AP isn't just "an access point." It's a configurable radio whose job changes per assignment. Local = the default, serves clients normally. FlexConnect = serves clients but switches traffic locally even if the WLC dies (branch offices love this). Sniffer / Monitor = no clients at all, the AP is now your security tool. Knowing which mode fits a stem is half the wireless exam.
