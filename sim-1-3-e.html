Fiber Mechanism: Why MMF, Why SMFdeep dive

You know one runs short and one runs long. This sim shows why — the physics underneath. Five reasons sit beneath "long vs short": modal dispersion, attenuation window, source cost, connector tolerance, bandwidth × distance product. Each panel below isolates one.

Cross-section — core size is the whole story

OM3/OM4 (50 µm): the workhorse inside data centers. Big core = LED/VCSEL works fine, connectors are forgiving, but light has many valid bounce angles ("modes") which causes modal dispersion (next panel).
core (light travels here)cladding (lower refractive index — bounces light back in)

Total internal reflection — what bounces inside the core

How it works: light hits the cladding boundary at a shallow angle and reflects fully back into the core (total internal reflection). The cladding has a slightly lower refractive index — that's the "wall." With a 50 µm core, an LED/VCSEL fills many valid angles. With a 9 µm core, geometry only allows one mode. One mode = no smearing.

Slide distance — watch the right pulse smear past the bit period (100 ps for 10G). Once it crosses, neighboring bits collide → ISI → bit errors.
Math: output_width = √(input² + (DMD × L)²). Input pulse = 100 ps (one bit at 10 Gbps). When output > 100 ps, the pulse leaks into the next bit slot → intersymbol interference → link fails. SMF dispersion at 1310 nm with a narrow DFB laser is so small it doesn't move the needle inside 10 km.
