Start With the Exam Objectives

Before anything else, read the official exam objectives. Know exactly what Cisco tests you on. Every page on this site maps back to these objectives — they are the source of truth.

Read Exam Objectives

If you only have 5 minutes, do this:

1Read the Exam Objectives — know exactly what's on the test.
2Take the Diagnostic (Practice menu) — it tells you exactly where you're weak.
3Check CORE — your weakest domain auto-expands. See your scores per topic.
4Start Daily Study (Practice menu) on your weakest domain.
5Join a Study Group — share your code with friends for accountability.
How scoring works

Every quiz answer, diagnostic question, lab completion, glossary quiz, and Packet Race question updates your CORE. The score uses an exponential moving average (EMA) — recent answers matter more than old ones. Your overall readiness is the weighted average across all 6 CCNA domains. The more you practice, the more accurate your score becomes.

What updates your score (and what doesn't)

Counts: Diagnostic, Quiz, Daily Study, Console Labs, PT Labs (Mark Complete), Glossary Quiz mode, Packet Race.
Doesn't count: Reading Learn Visually, browsing 3D Explorer/Topology, reading Objectives, reading Resources, viewing CORE. These are learning tools — the score only moves when you answer questions.

Your progress is safe

Auto-backed up: Your scores, quiz history, streaks, and study progress are automatically saved to the cloud every time you study. No action needed.

If you lose your data (cleared browser, new device, incognito): visit the site — it will detect empty progress and offer to restore from your cloud backup automatically.

In a Study Group? Your progress is also stored with your group. Go to Study Group, enter your group code + display name, and your full progress restores instantly.

Manual backup: You can also export your progress as a JSON file anytime from the CORE page footer (Export Progress button). Keep it as an extra safety net.

Workflow

Recommended Study Workflow

First time (Day 1)

1Exam Objectives — Read the full list. Know exactly what Cisco tests you on before you study anything.
2Diagnostic — Take the full assessment. Don't study first — it needs to measure your starting point honestly.
3CORE — Look at your results. Note which domains are red.
4Study Group — Create a group, share the code with friends studying for the same exam.
5Strategy — Read this once. It'll change how you study.
6Sprint or Learning Path — Pick your study plan based on your timeline.

Daily routine (20-60 min)

1Dashboard — Check your readiness and today's recommendation.
2Daily Study — Pick your weakest topic and do a focused session.
3Quiz — 10-15 questions on what you just studied. This is where learning sticks.
4Console Lab or PT Lab — Practice one hands-on lab. Commands need muscle memory.
5CORE — Check what moved. Celebrate progress.

Weekly (once a week)

1Study Group — Check the leaderboard, compare domain scores, read AI analysis.
2Packet Race — Race a friend on a config scenario. Makes studying competitive and fun.
3Glossary Quiz — 10 minutes of term drilling. Vocabulary matters on the exam.

Biweekly (every 2 weeks)

1Diagnostic retake — See how much you've grown. Compare to your last score.
2Adjust focus — If a domain didn't improve, switch your daily study to that domain exclusively.
Deep Dive

Structured Study Path (Start-to-Finish)

If you prefer a linear, organized approach — no jumping around, no guessing what to do next — follow this exact sequence. It takes 8-12 weeks depending on your pace.

Week 0 — Setup (1 day)

1Read the Exam Objectives — know what the test covers before you study a single topic.
2Read the Strategy page (Plan menu). Understand how to study effectively before you start.
3Take the Diagnostic (Practice menu). Don't study first — this measures your honest starting point.
4Open Study Group and create a group. Share the code with anyone studying for the CCNA.
5Set your exam date on the Dashboard. This unlocks the countdown timer.
6Install Cisco Packet Tracer (free from netacad.com). You'll need it starting Week 2.

Weeks 1-2 — Domain 1: Network Fundamentals

1Open Learning Path → study the foundation tier topics (OSI, TCP/IP, IP addressing).
2Read the Objectives page → Domain 1 section. Make sure you can explain each point.
3Study the visuals: Learn Visually → filter Domain 1. Study OSI model, TCP vs UDP, IPv4/IPv6.
4Use Daily Study on topics 1.1 through 1.13, one per session.
5Quiz yourself: Quiz → Domain 1 topics. 10 questions per topic minimum.
6Study the Subnetting Cheat Sheet. Practice the magic number method until it's automatic.
7Do Console Lab 01 (Navigate IOS Modes) and Lab 02 (Set a Hostname).
8Do PT Lab 0 (Getting Started with Packet Tracer) and PT Lab 3 (IPv6 + Inter-VLAN).

Weeks 3-4 — Domain 2: Network Access

1Objectives → Domain 2. VLANs, trunking, STP, EtherChannel, wireless.
2Learn Visually → filter Domain 2. Study VLAN tagging, STP states, EtherChannel.
3Daily Study + Quiz on topics 2.1-2.9.
4Console Labs 03-05 (VLAN, Port Assignment, Trunk).
5PT Lab 1 (Multi-VLAN) and PT Lab 2 (STP + EtherChannel).
6Open Devices page → click each access switch, distribution switch, and AP. Understand where each sits.
7Glossary → Quiz Mode. Drill 802 standards (802.1Q, 802.1D, 802.1w, 802.3af/at).

Weeks 5-6 — Domain 3: IP Connectivity

1Objectives → Domain 3. Routing, OSPF, static routes, default routes.
2OSPF Mastery page — work through the entire module. This is the hardest domain.
3Learn Visually → OSPF neighbor states, routing table, admin distances.
4Console Labs 06-07 (IP Address, Static Route) and Lab 09 (OSPF).
5PT Lab 4 (Multi-Router OSPF) and PT Lab 5 (Break and Fix OSPF).
6Quiz → Domain 3 topics extensively. This domain is worth ~25% of the exam.

Weeks 7-8 — Domains 4 & 5: IP Services + Security

1Objectives → Domains 4 and 5. NAT, DHCP, DNS, NTP, Syslog, SNMP, ACLs, AAA, port security.
2Learn Visually → NAT translation, ACL flow, DHCP DORA, QoS.
3Console Labs 08, 10, 11, 12 (SSH, ACL, Port Security, NAT).
4PT Lab 6 (NAT/DHCP) and PT Lab 7 (ACLs + Port Security).
5Devices page → click every server (DNS, DHCP, NTP, Syslog, AAA, TFTP). Read the exam tips.
6Daily Study + Quiz on all Domain 4 and 5 topics.

Week 9 — Domain 6: Automation & Programmability

1Objectives → Domain 6. SDN, REST APIs, JSON, YAML, Ansible, Terraform, DNA Center.
2PT Lab 8 (JSON + REST API Practice).
3Glossary → Quiz Mode. Drill automation terms (SDN, SD-WAN, NETCONF, RESTCONF, YANG).
4Quiz → Domain 6 topics. This domain is the lightest (10% of exam) but free points if you study it.

Weeks 10-11 — Review & Exam Prep

1Diagnostic retake — compare to your Week 0 score. You should be 70%+.
2CORE — any red topics? Attack them with targeted quizzes.
3Subnetting Mastery — timed drills. You need to subnet in under 30 seconds per question.
4Packet Race → play all 6 config scenarios. They simulate how packets move through a real network.
53D Topology → take the guided tour. Reinforce where every device and protocol lives.
6Objectives — go through every single one. Can you explain each to a friend? If not, study it.
7Resources → take a Boson ExSim practice exam ($99 but the closest thing to the real test).
For linear learners

Follow the structured path above exactly. Don't skip ahead. Don't jump to a "fun" topic. The domains are ordered by dependency — each builds on the last. Domain 1 (fundamentals) must come before Domain 3 (routing). Domain 2 (switching) must come before Domain 5 (security). Trust the sequence.
