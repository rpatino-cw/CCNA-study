IPv6 Unicast Address Classifier

IPv6 unicast addresses come in five flavors. The first few hex characters tell you which one. Type any IPv6 — the matching range lights up.
WHY this exists. Every IPv6 interface has a link-local (fe80::/10) address automatically. Most also get a global unicast (2000::/3, internet-routable). Unique-local (fc00::/7) is the IPv6 version of RFC 1918 — site-private. Loopback (::1) is localhost. Unspecified (::) means "no address yet" — used during DHCPv6 startup. Recognize the prefix, know the scope.
2001:db8::1 fe80::1 fc00::1 fd00:1234::abcd ::1 :: 2607:f8b0:4004::200e 3fff::1
