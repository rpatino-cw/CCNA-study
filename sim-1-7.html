Public vs Private IPv4 Classifier

Three address ranges are reserved for private networks (RFC 1918) — they're not routable on the public internet. Plus link-local (APIPA) and loopback. Type any IP — see which bucket it falls in.
WHY this exists. The public IPv4 address space is exhausted — there aren't enough to give every device its own. RFC 1918 carved out three ranges that anyone can reuse internally: 10/8 (16M hosts, big enterprise), 172.16/12 (1M hosts, medium), 192.168/16 (65K hosts, home/SOHO). NAT translates between private inside and public outside. APIPA (169.254) is what your laptop self-assigns when DHCP fails. Loopback (127.x) never leaves the host.
