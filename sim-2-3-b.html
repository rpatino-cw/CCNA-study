Why EtherChannel

Two switches with 4 cables between them: STP would block 3 of them — wasteful. EtherChannel bundles them into one logical link so STP sees one port. Bandwidth multiplies, and a single link failure stays invisible to upper layers. The protocol that negotiates the bundle is either LACP (IEEE 802.3ad, vendor-neutral, active/passive), PAgP (Cisco-only, auto/desirable), or static "on" (no negotiation — both sides must be configured the same or you get a spanning-tree loop). Default in real life: LACP active/active.
