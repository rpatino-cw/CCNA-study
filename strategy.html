The Modular Weak-Area Method

The fastest approach from multiple pass reports. You never waste time on what you already know.

Phase 1 — Day 1

Diagnostic Baseline

  1. Take a full diagnostic or practice test cold
  2. Score each of the 6 domains separately
  3. Rank domains from weakest to strongest

Take the diagnostic →

Phase 2 — The Sprint Cycles

Two-Domain Sprints

  1. Pick your 2 weakest domains
  2. Watch Jeremy's IT Lab videos for those topics
  3. Do Packet Tracer labs for each topic
  4. Anki flashcards daily (just those domains)
  5. Take a domain-specific quiz (not full exam)
  6. When scoring 80%+, move to the next 2 weakest
  7. Repeat until all 6 domains are above 80%

Quiz weak areas →

Phase 3 — Weeks 9-11

Full Exam Simulations

  1. Only take full practice exams after completing all domains
  2. Boson ExSim: don't do each exam more than twice (you start memorizing answers)
  3. Read every Boson explanation — even for questions you got right
  4. Target: 850+ consistently before booking the real exam

Speed Multipliers

From people who passed in 4-8 weeks.

01
Subnetting daily
15 min/day from Day 1. Target <30 sec per question. Worth 10-15% of the exam alone. Use subnettingpractice.com.
02
70% labs, 30% reading
The exam is command-heavy. If you can't type show ip ospf neighbor from muscle memory, you're not ready.
03
Active video watching
Pause, lab it, Anki it. One video per session with hands-on follow-up beats binging 10 videos passively.
04
Boson explanations > scores
Read every explanation — even for questions you got right. People credit Boson explanations as their single biggest score boost.
05
Your DCT advantage
You cable and rack daily — Domains 1 and 2 should be your fastest sprints. Spend heavy time on Domain 3 (25% of exam) and Domain 5.
06
Practice test threshold
Book the real exam only when consistently scoring 850+. One candidate booked after hitting 838 on Boson Exam C — the general advice is 840+ means ready.

Exam Day Checklist

  • Bring two forms of ID (one with photo)
  • Arrive 15 minutes early at Pearson VUE
  • No notes, phones, or watches in the testing room
  • You get a whiteboard — dump subnetting charts and OSPF states immediately
  • Flag hard questions and come back — don't burn 5 minutes on one question
  • 120 minutes, ~100-120 questions, passing score 825/1000
