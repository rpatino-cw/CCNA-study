NETWORK+ SHORTCUT

Network+ tests basic subnetting only: /8, /16, /24 and simple host calculations. You do NOT need VLSM, wildcard masks, or Cisco-level subnetting speed. Focus on sections 1-3 (binary, CIDR chart, magic number basics).

What you need: Convert /prefix to mask, count usable hosts (2^n - 2), identify network/broadcast. That's it.
Skip: VLSM, Binary AND, wildcard masks, speed optimization drills.

6. VLSM — Carve Different-Sized Subnets From One Block

Real networks need different subnet sizes. VLSM lets you split a /24 into chunks. Always allocate largest-first.

Example: Split 192.168.1.0/24 for 4 departments
VLSM Rule: Always allocate largest subnet first, then next largest, and so on. This prevents gaps and overlap.
