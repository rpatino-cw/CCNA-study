Three rules to remember

1
Headers on the front
Every layer adds its header to the FRONT. Only L2 adds a trailer (the 4-byte FCS) on the back.
2
MAC changes, IP never
Src/Dst MAC are rewritten at every router. Src/Dst IP and the ports stay frozen end to end.
3
dot1q lives inside L2
The 802.1Q tag sits inside the Ethernet header (after Src MAC), only on trunk links. TTL drops 1 per router.
